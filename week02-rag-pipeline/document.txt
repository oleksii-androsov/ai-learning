Acme Financial Services — Annual Report 2024

EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

Acme Financial Services delivered a strong performance in fiscal year 2024, achieving record revenue of €16.8 billion, representing a 14% increase year-over-year. This growth was driven by continued digital transformation initiatives, expansion into new European markets, and the successful deployment of AI-powered services across our core business lines.

Our customer base grew to 4.2 million active clients, up from 3.7 million in 2023. Customer satisfaction scores reached an all-time high of 91%, reflecting the impact of our investments in digital channels and personalized financial advisory services.

FINANCIAL PERFORMANCE

Revenue for fiscal year 2024 reached €16.8 billion, compared to €14.7 billion in 2023. Net profit increased by 18% to €2.4 billion. Operating costs were held flat at €11.2 billion despite inflationary pressures, due to efficiency gains from cloud migration and process automation.

Retail banking contributed €6.2 billion in revenue, representing 37% of total revenue. Corporate banking contributed €5.8 billion (35%), while wealth management accounted for €3.1 billion (18%). Insurance products and other services made up the remaining €1.7 billion (10%).

The cost-to-income ratio improved from 76% in 2023 to 67% in 2024, reflecting operational leverage from technology investments made over the prior three years.

DIGITAL TRANSFORMATION

2024 marked the completion of our three-year cloud migration program. All core banking systems are now running on AWS, with workloads distributed across eu-central-1 (Frankfurt) as primary region and eu-west-1 (Dublin) as disaster recovery. This migration reduced infrastructure costs by €340 million annually and improved system availability from 99.7% to 99.97%.

Mobile banking adoption reached 78% of active customers, up from 61% in 2023. Monthly active users on the mobile platform grew to 3.1 million. The average customer now completes 94% of transactions digitally, compared to 71% in 2021.

We launched our AI-powered personal finance assistant in Q2 2024. By year end, 1.2 million customers had activated the feature. The assistant uses large language models to provide personalized spending insights, savings recommendations, and proactive fraud alerts. Early data shows customers using the AI assistant save an average of €180 more per month than non-users.

RISK AND COMPLIANCE

Non-performing loan (NPL) ratio improved to 1.8%, down from 2.4% in 2023, reflecting disciplined underwriting standards and a favorable macroeconomic environment in our core markets.

Our AI-powered fraud detection system, deployed in Q3 2023, continued to demonstrate strong results. Fraud losses decreased by 47% in 2024 compared to 2022 baseline levels. The system processes 4.2 million transactions per day in real time, with an average decision latency of 38 milliseconds.

We completed our DORA (Digital Operational Resilience Act) compliance program in Q4 2024, ahead of the January 2025 regulatory deadline. This involved mapping and testing 847 critical ICT dependencies, establishing contractual requirements with 112 third-party technology providers, and implementing enhanced incident reporting procedures.

Capital adequacy ratio stood at 18.2%, well above the regulatory minimum of 10.5%. Liquidity coverage ratio was 142%, above the 100% regulatory requirement.

MARKET EXPANSION

In 2024 we expanded operations into Poland, Czech Republic, and Hungary, establishing retail banking presence in all three markets. Combined, these new markets contributed €420 million in revenue in their first year of operation, ahead of the €300 million target.

Our Polish operation launched in February 2024 with a digital-first model, requiring no physical branch infrastructure. Within 10 months, we acquired 180,000 customers in Poland, making it the fastest market entry in company history.

We also deepened our presence in existing markets. In Germany, we acquired regional savings bank Stadtsparkasse Rheinland for €680 million, adding 340,000 customers and 28 branch locations in the Rhine-Ruhr metropolitan area.

SUSTAINABILITY AND ESG

Acme Financial Services achieved carbon neutrality in its direct operations in 2024, two years ahead of our original 2026 target. This was accomplished through a combination of renewable energy procurement (100% of office and data center electricity), efficiency improvements, and high-quality carbon offset purchases for residual emissions.

We committed €8.5 billion in sustainable finance in 2024, including €3.2 billion in green bonds, €2.8 billion in sustainability-linked loans, and €2.5 billion in social finance initiatives targeting affordable housing and SME development in underserved communities.

Our workforce diversity metrics improved across all dimensions. Women now represent 44% of senior management roles, up from 38% in 2023. We introduced mandatory pay equity audits in all markets, resulting in targeted salary adjustments for 3.2% of employees.

TECHNOLOGY AND INNOVATION

R&D investment increased to €890 million in 2024, representing 5.3% of revenue. Key initiatives included the development of our proprietary credit scoring model using alternative data sources, expansion of our AI fraud detection capabilities to cover cryptocurrency transactions, and a proof-of-concept for blockchain-based cross-border payments.

We filed 34 technology patents in 2024, bringing our total portfolio to 127 active patents. Our technology team grew to 4,800 employees, representing 18% of total headcount.

We established an AI Center of Excellence in Frankfurt in Q1 2024, staffed by 120 data scientists and ML engineers. The center is responsible for developing and deploying AI models across all business lines, establishing AI governance frameworks, and ensuring compliance with emerging EU AI Act requirements.

OUTLOOK FOR 2025

We enter 2025 with strong momentum. Revenue guidance for 2025 is €18.5 to €19.2 billion, representing 10-14% growth. We expect continued improvement in the cost-to-income ratio, targeting 63% by year end.

Key priorities for 2025 include the launch of a GenAI-powered corporate banking advisory service, expansion of our AI assistant to business banking customers, completion of our ISO 42001 AI management system certification, and further market expansion into Romania and Bulgaria.

We will also begin preparations for potential IPO in 2026, subject to market conditions. Our investment banking advisors have been engaged and preliminary documentation work has commenced.

RISK FACTORS

The following risk factors could materially affect our performance in 2025. Macroeconomic deterioration in the Eurozone, particularly a recession in Germany, could increase NPL ratios and reduce lending volumes. Regulatory changes, including potential amendments to the EU AI Act or new capital requirements under Basel IV, could increase compliance costs. Cybersecurity threats continue to evolve; a major incident could disrupt operations and damage customer trust. Competitive pressure from fintech challengers and large technology companies entering financial services remains intense.
